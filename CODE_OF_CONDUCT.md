# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in the Email Failure Lab community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We will act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- Showing empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Respecting different opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully.
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing when appropriate, and learning from mistakes.
- Focusing on what is best for the community, not only for individuals.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Sexualized language or imagery, or sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

The project maintainer is responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and may take fair corrective action when behavior is inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

The maintainer may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. Reasons for moderation decisions will be communicated when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces and when someone officially represents the project in public spaces.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior to the project maintainer. For a private report, use the repository's [private reporting form](https://github.com/ralfferreira/email-failure-lab/security/advisories/new) and begin the summary with `Code of Conduct:`. Do not include sensitive details in a public issue.

If a report concerns the project maintainer or abuse hosted on GitHub, use [GitHub's abuse reporting channel](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse).

Reports will be reviewed fairly and with respect for the reporter's privacy and safety. The maintainer will determine consequences based on the context and impact of the behavior.

## Enforcement guidelines

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Inappropriate language or behavior that is unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private written warning that explains the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** A violation through a single incident or a series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. The person may be required to avoid interaction with the people involved for a defined period.

### 3. Temporary ban

**Impact:** A serious violation, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from project interaction and public communication for a defined period.

### 4. Permanent ban

**Impact:** A pattern of violations, sustained harassment, aggression, or disparagement of groups of people.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from project interaction and public communication.
